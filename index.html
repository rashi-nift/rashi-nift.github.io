About us
Narayanpet is a quintessential town and the district headquarters of Narayanpet district of the Indian state of Telangana, located 167 kms away from the capital, Hyderabad. A small town with the population of 50,000 people, it is known as the land of cotton and silk handloom sarees.

Known for their affordability, durability, low maintenance, simplicity and light weight nature, their borders and pallus are traditional and are characterised by a rich pallu with a unique pattern of alternating red and white bands. The borders are usually a flat expanse of deep maroon or chocolate red thinly separated by white or coloured lines. Narayanpet sarees are regarded as the garment of the Gods. These magnificent sarees enjoyed the royal patronage of the Marathas and were donned exclusively by the aristocrats.

Shreemulam was launched to prevent the dying craft of handloom sarees and to support the artisans who are surviving on meagre wages. The sarees made in the town are generally bought by the local population or sent to the nearby towns and cities for sale, and are sold at higher prices than they were purchased from the weavers, resulting in their loss.
